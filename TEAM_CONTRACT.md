## 3. Conflict Plan

### 3.1 Process for resolving disagreement
Team members will raise concerns respectfully during the daily sync or through the team's communication channels. Discussions will focus on technical reasoning, project requirements, and team consensus. If needed, a dedicated meeting will be scheduled to ensure all perspectives are heard before making a final decision.

### 3.2 If one teammate is dominating or steamrolling
A teammate will first have a respectful private conversation with the individual to explain the concern. If the behavior continues, the issue will be discussed openly during a team meeting. If no resolution is reached, the matter will be escalated following the agreed process: Support Instructor, followed by the Lead Instructor.

### 3.3 If one teammate is under-contributing or missing check-ins
The team will first contact the member privately to understand any challenges and offer support where possible. If participation does not improve, responsibilities and expectations will be reviewed during the team meeting. Continued lack of contribution will follow the agreed escalation path: Support Instructor, followed by the Lead Instructor.

### 3.4 Handling mismatched skill levels
The team will handle mismatched skill levels through coaching, pair programming, code reviews, and early communication. More experienced teammates guide and explain but do not take over the full task. Teammates who need help are expected to ask early and share what they've already tried – if stuck for more than 30–60 minutes, ask for help rather than disappearing into a rabbit hole.

### 6.2 Branch strategy
**Feature branch → main.** Every task is implemented on its own feature branch (`feature/<slug>` or `<handle>/<slug>`), followed by a Pull Request. Each PR requires review and approval before merging to main, per branch protection rules. No `dev` staging branch planned unless the team decides otherwise.

### 6.3 PR review workflow
Per the standard template: 2 teammate approvals required before merging to main, enforced via branch protection with no bypass; PR author does not merge their own PR; stale-approval dismissal is on.

### 6.4 Merge cadence
Our team will merge PRs daily during our team sync when possible, and on demand if a completed task is blocking other teammates. When a PR is ready for review, the author will notify the team in our communication channel and tag the required reviewers.

### 6.5 GitHub Project board - how your team runs it
- Columns: Backlog / In progress / In review / Done
- Every task is a card with an owner; no unassigned cards in In progress
- Automations: PR opened → In Review; PR merged → Done
- If a card sits in In progress for more than 24 hours without a note, it's raised at standup
- **Daily standup:** 10:00 AM (Jordan Time), 15 minutes maximum.
